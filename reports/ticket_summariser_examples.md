## Example 1: Account Abuse — Critical Severity

**Ticket ID:** TKT-0047
**Risk Category:** Account abuse
**Subcategory:** Account takeover
**Severity:** Critical
**SLA Target:** 1 hour
**Escalation Team:** Trust & Safety Review
**Human Review Required:** Yes

**Original Report:**
> "Someone is sending messages from my account. I did not authorise this. My password was changed and I cannot log back in. This started about an hour ago."

**Reviewer Summary:**

| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| Ticket summary | User reports unauthorised access to their account. Password has been changed without their knowledge. Active session compromise likely in progress. |
| Risk rationale | Immediate account takeover indicators present. Unauthorised password change and active unauthorised messaging are high-confidence signals of account compromise. |
| Recommended next step | Escalate to Trust & Safety Review immediately. Force account session termination. Initiate account recovery workflow. Review login event logs. |
| SLA note | Critical severity — action required within 1 hour. |
| Human review note | Human review mandatory. Active compromise risk. Do not automate final action. |

## How Summaries Are Generated

The summariser uses deterministic rule-based templates. It does not call a live AI API.

Each summary is generated from structured dataset fields:

- ticket_id, risk_category, subcategory, severity
- sla_target_hours, escalation_team, human_review_required
- user_report text (for the original report display only)

Templates vary by:

- risk category
- severity level
- human review flag
- escalation team assignment

This approach keeps the project fully reproducible and safe for public portfolio use.
